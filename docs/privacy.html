Moody Meals — Privacy

Beta (TestFlight). Last updated July 13, 2026.

Where your data lives

Everything you put into Moody Meals — meals, recipes, plans, shopping lists, household details — is stored on your device. If iCloud device backup is on, Apple includes the app's data in your encrypted device backup, under your Apple Account. A future version will sync your household's data through your own private iCloud (CloudKit) container; it stays yours and is never visible to us or anyone outside the people you invite.

What leaves your device

Today: nothing. The app makes no network requests, has no accounts, no analytics, no tracking, and no third-party SDKs.

A planned feature will send recipe text only (the recipe you're capturing) to Anthropic's Claude API to assess gluten-substitution needs. When it ships, the app will show what leaves the device, the results are stored back on your device, and nothing else — no names, no household details — is included.

Permissions

Calendar and Reminders access are optional, off by default, and used only to write your own planned dinners and shopping lists to your own device. Denying them changes nothing else about the app.

Contact

During the beta, use the TestFlight feedback button — it reaches the developer directly.
